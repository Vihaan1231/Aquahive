/ 01 — THE SCIENCE

The beetle knew it first.
We just engineered the panel.

The Namib desert beetle survives one of Earth's driest places by collecting fog on its bumpy back. AQUAHIVE replicates that architecture at building scale — combined with lotus-leaf self-cleaning surfaces.

Namib Beetle · Micro

Hydrophilic bumps attract water from vapour. Droplets nucleate and grow exactly where we want them — just like the beetle's back.

Lotus Leaf · Nano

Hydrophobic valleys guide every drop downward without loss, while a self-cleaning nano-texture filters airborne particulates.
